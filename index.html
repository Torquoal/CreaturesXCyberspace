Creatures x Cyberspace Workshop

Conference: MUM 2026 · Location: University of Glasgow · Room: TBD · Date: December

A Design Futures Workshop Scoping the Future of Online Interaction Between Animals

Banner Image (placeholder)

We bring together researchers from interaction design, remote technologies, and animal-computer interaction (ACI) to explore the future of how animals could interact with each other online using technology.

Animals in zoos, conservation settings, domestic care, or working roles often have limited opportunities for social interaction, whether within or across species. Research has shown that pets like parrots and dogs can connect via video calls, but the wider possibilities for how different animals could interact online, and how could vary for animals in the home, zoos, farms and beyond, are underunexplored.

Together, we aim to push these ideas further, imagining future scenarios, activities, and technologies, with animals centred as the users. We will also use design futuring to pre-empt the benefits, societal impacts, and ethical challenges of this paradigm shift.

Workshop outcomes will be gathered into key themes and insights that help shape the future of remote ACI research and design. We also plan to develop a post-workshop publication with optional engagement from attendees, supporting ongoing collaboration and community building in this emerging area.

If you’d like to take part, please sign up below for free. You’ll be asked for your name, area of expertise, and a short note on your hopes, ideas, and concerns about animals interacting online.
